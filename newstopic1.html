Davao 7.4 Earthquake

Three Earthquakes above magnitude 6.0 hit the Philippines within a span of 12 days, causing heavy property damage, grave injuries, and many deaths.

The first quake was in Cebu on September 30, 2025 with a magnitude of 6.9, the epicenter located just 21 kilometers away from Bogo City, Cebu.

The Earthquake costed approximately three billion pesos with some estimates reaching over four billion pesos.

The next one was in Davao on October 10 2025 with a magnitude of 7.4, the epicenter is located offshore near Manay, Davao Oriental

This caused residents to flee from their homes, and wide structural damage across Davao and other regions nearby.

The last of these three occurred on the same day, with a magnitude of 6.4, the epicenter being the same as the last.

This caused more structural damage, causing buildings that were barely standing to fall, the total cost of these earthquakes costing aproxiamately 2.26 billion pesos. These earthquakes cause mass destruction across the places affected, many people injured, some even died.
